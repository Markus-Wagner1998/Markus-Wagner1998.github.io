I started my career by studying Computer Science at the University of Passau. My studies presented me, that there are three main topics of Computer Science, that I am really into. These are Software Development, Software Testing and Archtitecture. In order to build up skills in all these fields and reassure, that this is what I want to do for the rest of my life, I started working as a Student Software Engineer at msg systems ag in Passau already during my first semester. My starting point was as a SAP / ABAP Developer in the Reinsurance Area, where I spent most of my Working Student Time. I finished my Bachelor's Degree in 2019 as Best of Year with a final Grade of 1,1. Afterwards I continued working at msg and moved into the field of automation and input management. Also in this field, my first steps began in the Reinsurance Area, where we developed a smartFIX Product called smartRIA to automate the processing of Reinsurance Accountings. These two products gave me lots of insights on the aspects of Software Engineering and Software Testing. I built lots of knowledge in these fields, but I also learned that I want to take part in design decisions, testing concepts and all the conceptional tasks, that are there. I don't want to be a pure programmer. As much insight as I got in the fields of Development and Testing, as few insights I got in the architectural decisions. In the SAP world I worked at more or less feature complete products, where no architectural decisions had to be made and smartFIX does not leave architecture decisions to you. This makes the next step in my career even more valuable. We started moving our Solutions to the Cloud. Which for one meant, that we had to design cloud architectures, because we started in a green field and two, that we built a new team where I could try to to fill a leading position. The next steps went as good as possible (or even better). Firtsly we started by migrating smartRIA to the Cloud by providing all its interfaces via Rest Endpoints and taking all the operations aspects. Here, together with a colleague of mine, I developed the architectural design and checked for technical doability. During the project, this colleague of mine and me took over the technical lead in that project by guiding other colleagues, making design decisions, managing our cloud infrastructure and onboarding new customers. With a first successful cloud project in our pockets we became more confident and started our own Cloud-Native Application Development. By not just migrating, but rebuilding one of our most successful Software Solutions, the Smart Table Upload, into the Cloud as a Service. If you are more interested in how we rebuilt the existing On-Premise Smart Table Upload in the Cloud (and if you are a german speaker) you can also listen to this Tech-Talk a colleague of mine and me gave during the msg series Cloud Insights. During this project, even more than before I took over technical leadership and am now, again together with my colleague, the leading developer, test manager and software architect in this project. All these projects gave me insights on where I want to go. If you ask me, where I see myself in 5 years, here is a short but concise answer. I still want to be a software engineer. However I love architecturing new (Cloud) software, which is hopefully a large part of my daily job by then. Furthermore, I really enjoy training new colleagues, especially young ones. I am already mentor for two working students and I don't want this to decrease in the future. If it's my decision, then this even increases in the future.
